During the summer of 2025, I had the incredible experience of interning at Pie Press Publishing, located in Johnstown, Pennsylvania. I learned so much about the publishing and writing world. My time here was spent doing an abundance of different things, such as making social media graphics that promoted StoryBundles, short stories, and podcast episodes, working with the software Vellum to update ebooks, creating a story bible for an upcoming novel, and doing research for a few different topics.

My favorite task I did while interning was research on the 1977 Johnstown flood, which will be used in an upcoming theatrical project by my supervisor. Johnstown is a short drive from my hometown, and it’s often referenced by other people when I try to explain where my hometown is.

“About 20 minutes north of Johnstown.”

“Oh yeah, the place with the floods.”

I have grown up hearing stories from my family about how the 1977 flood impacted them. The entire area was in turmoil from the natural disaster. I got my hands on original newspapers that date back to the flood in July 1977 from my grandparents, who kept them wrapped in plastic and stored them in their basement all these years. I was able to pull information from them for this research task, and I loved it. It felt particularly personal, an honor almost, to be reading a list of names of those who died in the flood from the same newspapers my grandparents held when reading that same list.
